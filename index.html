About

Combination of DFT and Machine Learning for the acceleration of homogeneous catalyst design

I am a third year PhD student currently completing my PhD in Computational Chemistry uner the supervision of Professor Tell Tuttle at the Unviersity of Strathclyde. In the intersection between quantum methods such as DFT and machine learning it can often be a challenge on how to approach the generation of data for such models. Over the course of my PhD i have built a wealth of experience in the calculation of transition state complexes and small molecules in order to be used as input data for machine learning models. This has afforded me a unique perspective on the problem of interspersing these two powerful technqiues into a singular approach capable of not only accurate predictions but those on a much smaller timescale than regular DFT calculations would afford.
